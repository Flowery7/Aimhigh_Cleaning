The Ultimate End of Tenancy Cleaning Checklist for South Devon Renters

Moving out of a rental property in Brixham, Paignton, Torquay or anywhere in South Devon can be stressful enough without worrying about whether you'll get your full deposit back. The truth is, most deposit disputes come down to cleaning standards. Landlords and letting agents have high expectations, and "good enough" often isn't good enough when it comes to the final inspection.

At Aim High Cleaning, we've helped hundreds of tenants across South Devon move out with confidence. We've put together this comprehensive end of tenancy cleaning checklist based on what landlords and agents in the Brixham and Torbay area actually look for during their final inspections.

Why End of Tenancy Cleaning Matters in South Devon

The South Devon rental market is competitive. Whether you're leaving a flat in Paignton, a cottage in Dartmouth or a house in Newton Abbot, your landlord will be preparing the property for the next tenant. A deep, professional-level clean is typically required by your tenancy agreement, and failure to meet the standard can result in deductions from your deposit — sometimes hundreds of pounds.

Professional end of tenancy cleaning in Brixham and the surrounding areas typically costs between £150 and £350 depending on the size of the property. Compare that to potential deposit deductions of £500 or more, and it's easy to see why so many South Devon tenants choose to book a professional service like Aim High Cleaning.

Room-by-Room End of Tenancy Cleaning Checklist

Kitchen

  • Oven: Deep clean inside and out, including racks, trays and the door glass. Remove all grease and burnt-on food.
  • Hob/Stovetop: Clean all burners, pan supports and surrounding surfaces. Remove grease buildup.
  • Extractor Hood: Clean filters and exterior surfaces.
  • Fridge/Freezer: Empty, defrost (if needed), clean inside and out. Remove all shelves and drawers and wash separately.
  • Microwave: Clean inside, outside and the turntable.
  • Sink and Taps: Descale and polish. Clean the overflow and plughole.
  • Worktops and Splashbacks: Clean all surfaces, removing grease and stains.
  • Cupboards and Drawers: Empty and clean inside and out, including handles.
  • Floor: Mop thoroughly, moving appliances to clean underneath where possible.
  • Windows: Clean inside glass and window sills.

Bathroom

  • Toilet: Clean inside, outside, behind and around the base. Descale if needed.
  • Bath/Shower: Remove all soap scum, limescale and mould. Clean tiles, grout and shower screen.
  • Sink and Taps: Descale and polish. Clean the overflow and plughole.
  • Mirrors: Clean and polish to a streak-free finish.
  • Tiles and Grout: Scrub grout lines and clean all tile surfaces.
  • Extractor Fan: Clean the cover and surrounding area.
  • Floor: Mop thoroughly, cleaning around the base of all fixtures.
  • Radiator: Dust and clean, including behind where accessible.

Bedrooms

  • Wardrobes and Cupboards: Empty and clean inside and out, including shelves and handles.
  • Windows: Clean inside glass, sills and frames.
  • Skirting Boards: Wipe down all skirting boards.
  • Light Fittings: Dust and clean light fittings and lampshades.
  • Switches and Sockets: Wipe clean.
  • Floors: Vacuum or mop thoroughly, including under beds and furniture.
  • Doors and Door Frames: Wipe down, including the tops of door frames where dust collects.

Living Room / Lounge

  • Windows: Clean inside glass, sills and frames.
  • Skirting Boards: Wipe down all skirting boards throughout.
  • Fireplace: Clean the hearth, surround and inside the fireplace if applicable.
  • Light Fittings: Dust and clean all fittings.
  • Switches and Sockets: Wipe clean.
  • Floors: Vacuum or mop thoroughly, moving furniture where possible.
  • Blinds and Curtains: Dust blinds or vacuum curtains.

Hallway and Stairs

  • Staircase: Vacuum or mop stairs, clean the banister and spindles.
  • Front Door: Clean inside and out, including the letterbox area.
  • Skirting Boards: Wipe down throughout.
  • Light Fittings: Dust and clean.
  • Floors: Vacuum or mop thoroughly.

Top Tips for South Devon Tenants

Start early: Don't leave cleaning until the last day. Give yourself at least two to three days to do a thorough job, or book a professional service like Aim High Cleaning well in advance of your move-out date.

Check your inventory: Review your original tenancy inventory to understand the standard the property should be returned to. This is your benchmark for the final clean.

Don't forget the small things: Light switches, door tops, window tracks, inside cupboards and behind toilets are the areas landlords check most carefully. These are the spots that separate a good clean from a great one.

Consider professional cleaning: If you're short on time or want guaranteed results, professional end of tenancy cleaning in Brixham from Aim High Cleaning gives you peace of mind. We know exactly what South Devon landlords expect, and we provide a checklist to prove every task has been completed.

How to Deep Clean Your South Devon Home: A Complete Room-by-Room Guide

Regular weekly cleaning keeps your Brixham or Torquay home looking tidy, but every now and then your home needs a proper deep clean — the kind that gets into every corner, behind every appliance, and above every cupboard. Whether you're preparing for spring, getting ready for guests, or just want to refresh your living space, this comprehensive guide from Aim High Cleaning will walk you through exactly how to deep clean your South Devon home from top to bottom.

Living in coastal South Devon comes with its own unique cleaning challenges. Salt air, sand, higher humidity and the beautiful but muddy countryside all take their toll on our homes. That's why a thorough deep clean at least twice a year is essential for homeowners in Brixham, Paignton, Dartmouth and the surrounding areas.

Before You Start: Gather Your Supplies

Having the right tools makes all the difference. Here's what we recommend having on hand before you begin your deep clean:

  • Microfibre cloths (multiple — for different rooms)
  • All-purpose cleaner (eco-friendly preferred)
  • Glass cleaner
  • Disinfectant
  • Bicarbonate of soda and white vinegar (natural cleaning powerhouses)
  • A good quality vacuum cleaner with attachments
  • Mop and bucket
  • Scrubbing brushes (stiff and soft)
  • Rubber gloves
  • Step stool (for reaching high places)
  • Bin bags

General Deep Cleaning Principles

Work top to bottom: Always start at the highest point in each room and work your way down. Dust and dirt fall, so if you clean the floor first and then dust the shelves, you'll just have to do the floor again.

Work left to right: This ensures you don't miss any areas. Systematically move around the room.

Tackle one room at a time: It's more efficient and gives you a sense of accomplishment as each room is completed.

Open your windows: Especially important in South Devon's coastal homes where moisture can build up. Good ventilation helps with drying and improves air quality while you clean.

Kitchen Deep Clean

The kitchen is often the room that benefits most from a deep clean. Here's how to do it properly:

Inside appliances: Pull out the fridge, oven, washing machine and dishwasher where possible and clean behind and underneath. Clean the rubber seals on your washing machine and dishwasher — these collect mould and grime. Run an empty cycle with a dishwasher cleaner or white vinegar.

Cupboard interiors: Empty every cupboard and drawer. Wipe down the interiors, including the tops of cupboards where dust and grease accumulate. Line shelves with fresh liner if needed.

Extractor hood and filters: Remove the filters from your cooker hood and soak them in hot, soapy water with bicarbonate of soda. Clean the exterior thoroughly.

Taps and sinks: Use white vinegar to descale taps. Clean the overflow hole in your sink — it's one of the dirtiest spots in any kitchen. Polish stainless steel sinks with a microfibre cloth and a little bicarbonate of soda.

Behind and under: Move the fridge, washing machine and any other movable appliances. You'll be surprised what finds its way under there — especially in busy South Devon family homes.

Bathroom Deep Clean

South Devon's coastal air means bathrooms in Brixham and Torquay homes can be prone to mould and limescale. Here's how to tackle both:

Limescale removal: Soak toiletries and showerheads in white vinegar overnight. For taps, wrap a vinegar-soaked cloth around them and leave for an hour before scrubbing. For stubborn limescale on shower screens, use a dedicated limescale remover or a paste of bicarbonate of soda and vinegar.

Grout cleaning: Make a paste of bicarbonate of soda and water, apply to grout lines, spray with white vinegar and let it fizz. Scrub with an old toothbrush. For really stained grout, you may need a dedicated grout cleaner — or call in the professionals at Aim High Cleaning.

Mould prevention: After cleaning mould from sealant and tiles, ensure the room is well ventilated. In South Devon's humid coastal climate, running the extractor fan during and after showers is essential. Consider using a mould-resistant sealant when resealing around baths and showers.

Shower curtain or screen: Wash fabric shower curtains in the washing machine. For glass screens, use a squeegee after every shower to prevent water marks and limescale buildup.

Living Room and Bedroom Deep Clean

Upholstery: Vacuum sofas and chairs thoroughly, including under cushions. Spot-clean any stains. For a professional-level clean, consider our carpet and upholstery cleaning service — we serve all of South Devon from Brixham to Totnes.

Curtains and blinds: Vacuum curtains with the upholstery attachment. Wash machine-washable curtains. For blinds, use a microfibre cloth or a dedicated blind-cleaning tool.

Under furniture: Move all furniture and clean underneath. This is one of the most commonly missed areas during regular cleaning.

Light fittings and ceiling fans: Dust accumulates on top of light fittings and fans. Use a microfibre cloth on an extendable duster to reach high fittings safely.

Skirting boards and door frames: Wipe down all skirting boards, door frames and the tops of doors. These areas collect dust that's often invisible until you look closely.

Windows and Glass

South Devon homes often have beautiful sea views — make sure your windows are clean enough to enjoy them. Clean both inside and outside glass using a glass cleaner or a solution of water and white vinegar. Use a squeegee for the best streak-free finish. Don't forget to clean the window sills, tracks and frames.

Eco-Friendly Cleaning Tips for Your South Devon Home: A Guide from Aim High Cleaning

South Devon is one of the most beautiful parts of the UK. From the stunning coastline of Brixham and Torquay to the rolling countryside of the South Hams, we're surrounded by natural beauty that's worth protecting. That's why at Aim High Cleaning, we're committed to using eco-friendly cleaning products and methods that are tough on dirt but gentle on the environment.

But you don't have to be a professional cleaner to make a difference. Here are our top eco-friendly cleaning tips that any South Devon homeowner or tenant can start using today — whether you live in a Brixham flat, a Dartmouth cottage or a Newton Abbot family home.

Why Eco-Friendly Cleaning Matters in South Devon

Many conventional cleaning products contain chemicals that can harm the environment. When these chemicals go down the drain, they can end up in our rivers, streams and eventually the sea — the same sea that makes South Devon such a special place to live. Coastal communities like Brixham, Paignton and Torquay have a particular responsibility to protect our marine environment.

Eco-friendly cleaning isn't just better for the planet — it's better for your health too. Harsh chemicals can cause skin irritation, respiratory problems and allergic reactions. If you have children, pets or anyone in your household with sensitivities, switching to natural cleaning products can make a real difference to their wellbeing.

The Natural Cleaning Powerhouses

You'd be amazed what you can achieve with just a few simple, natural ingredients. Here are the eco-friendly cleaning essentials that we use at Aim High Cleaning and recommend to all our South Devon clients:

White Vinegar

White vinegar is one of the most versatile natural cleaners available. It's a natural disinfectant, deodoriser and degreaser. Use it to clean windows, descale taps, remove limescale from showerheads, clean worktops and freshen up drains. Mix equal parts water and white vinegar in a spray bottle for an all-purpose cleaner. Don't use it on natural stone surfaces like marble or granite, as the acid can damage them.

Bicarbonate of Soda

Bicarbonate of soda is a gentle abrasive that's perfect for scrubbing surfaces without scratching. Use it to clean ovens, sinks, baths and tiles. It's also a natural deodoriser — sprinkle it on carpets, leave for 15 minutes and vacuum up to remove odours. For a powerful drain cleaner, pour bicarbonate of soda down the drain followed by white vinegar. Let it fizz for 15 minutes, then flush with boiling water.

Lemon Juice

Lemon juice is a natural bleach and deodoriser. Use it to clean chopping boards, remove stains from worktops and brighten up stainless steel sinks. Rub half a lemon on taps to remove water marks and leave them sparkling. Lemon juice mixed with bicarbonate of soda makes an excellent scrub for tough stains.

Castile Soap

Castile soap is a plant-based soap that's biodegradable and free from synthetic chemicals. Dilute it with water for an all-purpose floor cleaner, or use it for washing up. It's gentle enough for most surfaces and effective at cutting through grease.

Essential Oils

Essential oils like tea tree, lavender and eucalyptus have natural antibacterial properties. Add a few drops to your homemade cleaning solutions for extra cleaning power and a fresh, natural scent. Tea tree oil is particularly effective against mould and mildew — a common issue in South Devon's coastal homes.

Eco-Friendly Cleaning Tips for Every Room

Kitchen

  • Clean worktops with a solution of equal parts water and white vinegar
  • Deodorise your fridge by placing an open box of bicarbonate of soda inside
  • Clean your oven by making a paste of bicarbonate of soda and water, spreading it inside and leaving overnight before wiping clean
  • Use lemon juice to clean and freshen your microwave — heat a bowl of water with lemon slices for 3 minutes, then wipe down
  • Clean stainless steel appliances with a microfibre cloth and a small amount of olive oil

Bathroom

  • Remove limescale from taps by wrapping them in a white vinegar-soaked cloth for an hour
  • Clean shower screens with a solution of water and vinegar to prevent water marks
  • Use bicarbonate of soda as a gentle scrub for baths, sinks and tiles
  • Prevent mould by keeping the bathroom well-ventilated and wiping down wet surfaces after use
  • Clean the toilet with bicarbonate of soda and white vinegar — it's just as effective as chemical cleaners

Living Areas and Bedrooms

  • Dust with a damp microfibre cloth instead of chemical sprays — it traps dust rather than spreading it
  • Freshen carpets by sprinkling bicarbonate of soda, leaving for 15 minutes and vacuuming
  • Clean windows with a solution of water and white vinegar for a streak-free finish
  • Use essential oil diffusers instead of chemical air fresheners
  • Wash bedding at 60°C to kill dust mites naturally

Reducing Waste While Cleaning

Eco-friendly cleaning isn't just about the products you use — it's also about reducing waste. Here are some tips:

  • Use reusable cloths instead of disposable wipes. Microfibre cloths are excellent — they clean effectively with just water and can be washed and reused hundreds of times.
  • Buy in bulk to reduce packaging waste. Bicarbonate of soda and white vinegar are available in large quantities at low cost.
  • Make your own cleaning products using the simple recipes above. This eliminates single-use plastic bottles.
  • Choose concentrated products when you do buy commercial cleaners. They use less packaging and last longer.
  • Use a refillable spray bottle for your homemade cleaning solutions.
